Dubai, UAE


Dubai transforms into a magical wonderland during Eid celebrations, blending modern luxury with Islamic traditions. The city comes alive with spectacular fireworks over the world's tallest structures, lavish iftar banquets at 7-star hotels, and bustling souks filled with festive sweets and gifts. From spiritual prayers at Jumeirah Mosque to extravagant feasts along Sheikh Zayed Road, Dubai offers an unforgettable Eid experience for families and travelers worldwide.

  1. Burj Khalifa Fireworks: Witness *Dubai's iconic tower* light up with spectacular Eid displays and synchronized lights.
  2. Dubai Mall: Explore the world's largest mall during ***Eid shopping festivals*** with exclusive deals and gold souk treasures.
  3. Jumeirah Mosque: Join guided tours and Taraweeh prayers at this stunning ***architectural masterpiece*** open to all faiths.
  4. Burj Al Arab: Indulge in lavish ***Eid brunches*** at the world's only 7-star hotel with Arabian Gulf views.
  5. Global Village: Experience *international pavilions* showcasing 90 cultures with Eid fireworks and cultural performances.
  • Seviyan: *Sweet vermicelli pudding* cooked with milk, cardamom, and garnished with almonds and pistachios.
  • Luqaimat: *Crispy golden dumplings* drenched in date syrup, a traditional Emirati Eid dessert.
  • Mutton Mandi: *Slow-cooked tender lamb* served with fragrant rice and aromatic spices in underground clay ovens.
  • Sheer Khurma: *Creamy vermicelli milk pudding* infused with dates, saffron, and crushed nuts for festive indulgence.
  • Knafeh: *Cheesy sweet pastry* layered with shredded phyllo dough and soaked in rosewater syrup.
