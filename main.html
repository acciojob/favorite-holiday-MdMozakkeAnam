Eid

Eid, commonly referring to Eid al-Fitr and Eid al-Adha, are the two major Islamic festivals celebrated by Muslims worldwide. Eid al-Fitr marks the end of the holy month of Ramadan, a period of fasting, with communal prayers, feasting on sweets like seviyan, family gatherings, new clothes, and exchanging "Eid Mubarak" greetings, typically lasting three days after the new moon sighting. Eid al-Adha, the Festival of Sacrifice, commemorates Prophet Ibrahim's devotion by sacrificing an animal and sharing the meat with family, friends, and the needy, coinciding with the Hajj pilgrimage's culmination on the 10th of Dhu al-Hijja. Both holidays follow the lunar Hijri calendar, so their dates shift annually on the Gregorian calendar, emphasizing themes of charity, gratitude, and community.
